# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Pawgress is for people who share responsibility for a puppy, especially
households coordinating care across busy days. They use it in short bursts to
record something that just happened and in calmer moments to review routines,
health, training, and progress. The interface must work equally well in English
and Slovak.

## Product Purpose

Pawgress keeps a puppy's daily care in one dependable shared record. It should
make common updates quick to log, make recent activity easy to understand, and
help everyone in the household feel confident that important care has been
noticed and coordinated.

## Positioning

A well-kept shared household logbook that makes puppy care easy to record and
reassuring to understand.

## Brand Personality

Warm, reassuring, and intentional. Pawgress should feel calm and encouraging
without becoming childish, decorative, or precious. Personality appears in
thoughtful language and a few brand moments; routine product work stays clear
and familiar.

## Anti-references

Pawgress must not resemble an AI-generated lifestyle dashboard: pills used for
every control, giant editorial product headings, tiny uppercase tracked kickers,
oversized rounded cards, borders paired with broad shadows, nested cards,
decorative rotations, dot fields, or ornamental badges and icon bubbles. It
also avoids clinical pet-record software that feels cold or punitive.

## Design Principles

1. **Make the next action obvious.** Frequent care logging should take little
   interpretation, with one clear primary action per task area.
2. **Keep repetition calm.** Consistent hierarchy and familiar controls should
   make the interface quieter and easier with continued use.
3. **Show what matters now.** Time, status, sequence, exceptions, and progress
   receive emphasis before containers or decoration.
4. **Earn every warm detail.** Color and personality should reassure the user
   while preserving the clarity of a dependable household record.
5. **Design for the whole household.** Every flow must tolerate mobile use,
   longer names and notes, English and Slovak copy, and shared or changing data.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Preserve visible keyboard focus, semantic controls, usable
loading and error announcements, and non-color state cues. Support reduced
motion, a minimum viewport width of 320px, touch targets of at least 44px for
primary interactions, text zoom, and content expansion in both locales.
